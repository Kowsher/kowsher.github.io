About Me

Student Researcher

I am currently a student researcher at Tsinghua University. My core research interest lies in leveraging graph neural networks (GNNs) for modeling complex systems. I am particularly interested in geometrically equivariant GNNs which are powerful tools for learning the interactions in complicated physical/chemical systems in a highly data-efficient fashion.

Research Highlights

Equivariant Graph Mechanics Networks with Constraints (ICLR 22)

We develop Graph Mechanics Network (GMN), a powerful graph neural network particularly favorable for modeling constrained dynamical systems. GMN is E(n)-equivariant and thus enjoys very high data-efficiency and achieves promising performance on various 3D dynamics prediction datasets, ranging from molecules to human motion capture data.

Paper Code Poster OpenReview BibTex


Equivariant Graph Hierarchy-based Neural Networks (NeurIPS 22)

We propose Equivariant Graph Hierarchy-based Neural Networks (EGHNs) which incorporate learnable pooling scheme into equivariant message-passing graph neural networks for modeling complex physical or chemical systems at scale (e.g., proteins). The model also leverages Equivariant Matrix Message Passing (EMMP) modules as powerful backbones, enjoying theoretical guarantee of stronger expressivity than existing scalarization based EGNs.

Paper Code


Learning Physical Dynamics with Subequivariant Graph Neural Networks (NeurIPS 22)

We introduce Subequivariant Graph Neural Networks (SGNN) that relaxes equivariance to subequivariance by considering external fields like gravity, where the universal approximation ability holds theoretically; incorporates a new subequivariant object-aware message passing; operates in a hierarchical fashion, allowing for modeling long-range interactions.
